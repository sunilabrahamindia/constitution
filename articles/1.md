**Constitutional Text — Article 1**

**(1)** *India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States.*

**(2)** *The States and the territories thereof shall be as specified in the First Schedule.*

**(3)** *The territory of India shall comprise —*  
**(a)** *the territories of the States;*  
**(b)** *the Union territories specified in the First Schedule; and*  
**(c)** *such other territories as may be acquired.*

</div>

<div class="section" markdown="1">

### Lead summary

- Dual nomenclature: **India** / **Bharat**.  
- Constitutional form: **Union of States** (permanent, non-secessionist).  
- Territorial catalogue: States and Union Territories (First Schedule).  
- Clause (3) allows lawful territorial acquisition.

</div>

<div class="section" markdown="1">

### Short explanation

Article 1 sets the constitutional premise for India’s territorial unity. The terminology — *Union of States* — was chosen to avoid any implication that the Union is a voluntary compact of sovereign units. Clause (3) offers an adaptable description of territory so the constitutional system can accommodate internal reorganisation and lawful acquisitions.

</div>

<div class="section" markdown="1">

### Historical note

Constituent Assembly debates show careful negotiation between tradition and international recognition when settling on the dual name. Framers preferred language that emphasised unity and permanence while allowing administrative flexibility.

</div>

<div class="section" markdown="1">

### Key judicial references

- **Re: Berubari Union (1960)** — transfer/cession of Indian territory to another sovereign requires constitutional amendment under Article 368.  
- **State reorganisation jurisprudence** — confirms Parliament's broad powers under Articles 2–4 for internal reorganisation.

</div>

<div class="section" markdown="1">

### Cross references

- **Article 2** — Admission/establishment of new States.  
- **Article 3** — Formation/alteration of States (mechanism for internal reorganisation).  
- **First Schedule** — Current list of States and Union Territories.

</div>
